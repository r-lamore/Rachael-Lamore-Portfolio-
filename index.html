01 ¬∑ The Problem

Human reading only moves so fast.

Our research process was sound ‚Äî but it had a throughput ceiling. Gaps in market awareness were only surfacing after testing had already started, forcing expensive retests and slowing publishing pipelines.

Traditional Workflow

How we started every review

We'd analyze products our competitors praised, identify high-sellers, and scan for anything new or notable. The team brought real expertise. But even a great team has bandwidth limits ‚Äî and those limits were showing up at the worst possible moments.

Where friction accumulated

The failure modes we kept hitting

  • A key product missed at the lineup stage meant a retest ‚Äî after gear was already purchased and tested.
  • Test plans lacked consistency across team members, creating downstream confusion for writers.
  • No shared documentation meant different people were optimizing for different things.
  • Market coverage depended too heavily on whoever had the most time to read that week.
02 ¬∑ The Solution

Three prompts that protect research capital.

Rather than automating judgment, I used AI to fill the gaps where human bandwidth was the bottleneck. Each prompt serves a distinct purpose in the pre-fieldwork, lineup, and consumer-framing stages.

Prompt 01 Test Methodology
How should we actually test this?
What is the best way, in both a qualitative and quantitative fashion, to test [product]? Use methods that regular people will easily understand and relate to, whose outputs will be equally straightforward and relatable.

Forces the test plan to stay grounded in real consumer experience, not internal jargon. Keeps our rigorous data meaningful to a busy person skimming a review at 10pm.

Prompt 02 Product Lineup & SEO
What belongs in this lineup ‚Äî and what's just taking up space?
What are the most important products that need to be included in a top-of-search ranking review? Evaluate the current lineup of [category] and tell me what products are still current and which are outdated. Please include some potential award ideas along with the product list and evaluate what could be done to improve search rank and traffic: [URL]

This prompt does double duty: it validates the lineup before purchasing and flags gaps in our SEO footprint. It's the one that most directly prevents the costly retest scenario.

Prompt 03 Consumer Purchase Criteria
What does someone actually need to know before they buy?
What are the most critical factors for a consumer to know before they purchase [product] and what are the key purchase decisions?

Grounds the test plan in what readers are actually trying to figure out ‚Äî not what we find technically interesting. Every data point we collect should answer a question someone is genuinely asking.

05 ¬∑ Implications

What this tells us about AI in editorial work.

The most interesting finding wasn't efficiency. It was what the AI reflected back about what we were already doing ‚Äî and what we'd quietly drifted from.

I
AI as a mirror, not just a tool

A lot of what the AI recommended mirrored strategy we'd already implemented ‚Äî and reminded us where we'd quietly drifted from it. I'd felt for a while that our reviews were getting too dense: too many charts, too much data-driven jargon. The data validated our credibility, but it wasn't always the most useful output for a busy consumer trying to make a purchase decision.

The right answer is a balance ‚Äî objective numbers paired with clear writing that explains why those numbers matter to the reader. Using Prompt 02 in particular, the whole team got a concrete reminder of that nuance and collectively refocused. That kind of alignment is hard to manufacture in a meeting. Seeing it surfaced through a shared process made it stick differently.

II
The balancing act of product selection

In broad product categories, the lineup decision is where everything downstream gets determined. Miss a product someone is searching for and you bounce them off the page. Include too many and you bloat the testing and writing process ‚Äî both of which are real costs.

The AI analysis provides guard rails without being prescriptive. It captures the items we need while streaming us away from products that would dilute the review without adding value. The judgment call still belongs to the team ‚Äî but now we're making it with better information, earlier, when it's still cheap to change course.
